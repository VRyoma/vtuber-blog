"I want to set up my gear, but I don't know where to start." Every new streamer hits this wall. Search online and you'll find setups costing hundreds of thousands of yen. It's overwhelming.

This article sorts out the **three items worth buying first** from the **gear you can skip for now**. Budget-based combos are included so you can build a shopping list today.

**What you'll learn:**

- The three pieces of gear to prioritize and how to pick them
- What not to buy yet, plus free alternatives
- Recommended setups for 10k / 30k / 50k yen budgets
- Three things to check before you hit "buy"

## Start With These Three: Mic, Light, Webcam

How you look and sound directly affects how long viewers stick around. Out of everything, focus your budget on these three.

### 1. Microphone (Budget: 5,000–15,000 yen)

Audio problems are more noticeable than video problems. Even a little noise can make viewers tune out.

**What to look for:**

- **USB connection** — no audio interface needed
- **Cardioid (unidirectional) pattern** — picks up less keyboard noise from behind
- **Built-in gain dial** — lets you adjust volume without opening PC settings

A USB cardioid mic around 5,000 yen is plenty to get started. Step up to the 10,000–15,000 yen range and you'll get 48 kHz sampling and a shock mount.

### 2. Lighting (Budget: 2,000–5,000 yen)

Lighting makes or breaks how your face or avatar looks. Ceiling lights alone leave shadows under your eyes and wash out colors.

**What to look for:**

- **Ring light** — gives even illumination from a single source, great for beginners
- **Adjustable color temperature** (roughly 3,000–6,500 K) — switch between daylight and warm tones
- **Place it about 45 degrees in front of the camera** for natural-looking shadows

Even a 2,000 yen desk ring light can make your face two to three times brighter than ceiling lighting alone.

### 3. Webcam or Capture Card (Budget: 5,000–15,000 yen)

**For PC streaming**, pick up a 1080p webcam. 720p works, but the gap shows in thumbnails and archived recordings.

**For console streaming**, you'll need a capture card. Even if your console can stream directly, overlaying comments on screen usually requires passing through a capture card.

**What to look for:**

- Minimum resolution: **1080p at 30 fps**
- 1080p at 60 fps models start around 7,000 yen
- For capture cards, pick a **UVC-compliant** one — no driver install needed

### Priority Summary

| Priority | Gear | Budget Range | Can You Substitute? |
|---|---|---|---|
| High | Microphone | 5,000–15,000 yen | No (phone mics sound noticeably worse) |
| High | Lighting | 2,000–5,000 yen | Partly (desk lamp works, but looks rougher) |

## What You Don't Need Yet

Some gear that shows up in setups online isn't necessary when you're just starting. Try the free alternatives below first. Upgrade only when you feel the need.

### Stream Deck

A Stream Deck lets you switch scenes or play BGM with a single button. Handy, but OBS hotkeys or free smartphone apps like **Touch Portal** or **Macro Deck** do the same thing.

### Expensive Audio Mixers

Audio interfaces and mixers above 50,000 yen make sense once you need fine control over multiple inputs. With a single USB mic, your PC's volume mixer is enough.

### Green Screen

Green screens (chroma key) are cloth or panel backdrops for removing your background. Modern streaming software now includes **built-in background removal**. It's less precise than a proper green screen, but it costs nothing to try.

### High-End Camera

A webcam in the 10,000 yen range delivers solid quality. Save 4K cameras and mirrorless rigs for when your channel grows and you're ready for a serious upgrade.

### Multi-Light Setup

Three or four lights can boost production quality. But one ring light is enough at first. If your lower half looks dark, add a 1,000 yen LED panel and call it done.

### When to Buy: Quick Reference

| Gear | Free Alternative | When to Buy |
|---|---|---|
| Stream Deck | OBS hotkeys, phone apps | When you control 8+ items mid-stream |
| Expensive audio mixer | PC volume mixer | When you need 2+ mic inputs |
| Green screen | Software background removal | When the built-in effect isn't clean enough |
| High-end camera | 10k yen webcam | When viewers start asking for better video quality |
| Multi-light setup | Ring light + small LED | Once your room layout is locked in |

## Three Things to Check Before You Buy

Before adding gear to your cart, run through this quick checklist. It prevents impulse buys and buyer's remorse.

### 1. Find the Weakest Link in Your Current Setup

Record one minute of your stream, then watch it back objectively. Dark video? Get a light. Muffled audio? Get a mic. Blurry picture? Get a webcam. Pour your budget into the worst bottleneck. If your audio is fine but your face is dark, a single 2,000 yen ring light is the right call.

### 2. Look for Reviews from Streamers

General reviews often evaluate mics for music or meetings. For streaming-specific noise handling and avatar compatibility, you want feedback from people who actually stream. Search "streaming mic comparison" and you'll find plenty of creator-made review videos.

### 3. Buy from Stores with Easy Returns

Maybe the mic doesn't fit your desk. Maybe it picks up more noise than expected. These things happen. Choose shops with a 30-day return window, or buy in person. Stores that accept returns even after opening give you peace of mind to test things out.

## Recap

- Pour your budget into a **mic, light, and webcam** first — that's the most efficient use of your money
- Stream Decks and expensive mixers have **free alternatives, so wait until you feel the need**
- You can start streaming with a single mic and a 10k yen budget. **Start first, upgrade later** — you'll improve faster that way

**Try this today:** Record a one-minute test stream on your phone. Find the one thing that bothers you most — video, audio, or lighting. That's where your first investment goes.
