Ever posted "stream starting now!" on X and barely got any reactions?

If you announce your stream only once, most followers will miss it. Timelines move fast, and a single post can disappear in minutes.

This article walks you through a **three-post rhythm** from 30 minutes before your stream to the moment you go live, plus templates for each post.

**What you'll learn:**
- A three-post timing template with what to write at each step
- How to check when your followers are actually online
- The role of each post: tease, remind, and drive clicks

---

## Why one post isn't enough — the three-post rhythm

If you only post when the stream starts, anyone not scrolling at that exact moment won't see it. X timelines refresh in minutes, so spreading your announcement across multiple posts gives you more chances to be seen.

### What each post does

| Timing | Role | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 30 min before | Tease | Let people know something is coming |
| 5 min before | Reminder | Push fence-sitters to show up |
| Right at start | Launch | Give them a link to click |

The third post carries the link so the gap between seeing your post and joining your stream is as short as possible. Posts one and two build awareness. Post three drives action.

### How it looks on a timeline

```
20:30  "Going live at 21:00 with a zatsudan (free-talk stream)! Today's theme is..."
20:55  "5 minutes until we start! Come hang out!"
21:00  "We're live! 👇" + link
```

Someone might see the 20:30 post, think "nice, 21:00," and then forget. The 20:55 reminder jogs their memory, and the 21:00 link lets them jump in right away.

## Post templates — what to write at each step

Each of the three posts follows a set pattern. You don't need to start from scratch every time.

### 30 minutes before: the tease

**Structure:** Greeting + content summary + start time + hook

**Template example:**
> Hey everyone! Tonight at 21:00 I'm doing a zatsudan (free-talk stream).
> The theme: "Things I've been into lately."
> Drop your own answers in chat!
> Starts at 21:00

**Tips:**
- Summarize what the stream is about in one line
- Invite participation with a quick prompt like "let me know in chat"
- Keep emojis to one or two

### 5 minutes before: the reminder

**Structure:** Short alert + waiting signal

**Template example:**
> 5 minutes to go!
> See you soon 👇

**Tips:**
- Keep it short enough to catch while scrolling
- No link yet — save that for the launch post

### Right at start: the launch

**Structure:** "We're live" + stream link + one-line hook

**Template example:**
> We're live!
> Tonight: one indie game, one shot at clearing it.
> 👇 Come watch
> [stream link]

**Tips:**
- **Always include the link** — this is your one click-driving post
- Add a one-line highlight to nudge anyone on the fence
- Use a format that generates an X link preview card

## Find when your followers are actually online

Once the three-post rhythm is in place, check **when your followers are most active**. Evenings (20:00-22:00) are common, but your audience might peak in the morning or midday.

### How to check X Analytics

1. Open X and go to More > Analytics
2. Switch to the Audience tab
3. Look at the "followers online hours" chart

The peaks on that chart show when your followers are browsing X. Line up your announcements with those windows and more people will see them.

### Adjust your start time to match

If your followers are most active from 21:00 to 23:00, starting at 21:00 means all three posts land inside that window.

| Peak hours | Stream start | 30 min before | 5 min before |
|---|---|---|---|
| 20:00-22:00 | 20:00 | 19:30 | 19:55 |
| 21:00-23:00 | 21:00 | 20:30 | 20:55 |
| 12:00-14:00 | 12:00 | 11:30 | 11:55 |

You don't need every stream at the same time. Even shifting half of your weekly streams to match your peak window can make a noticeable difference.

## Don't overdo it — what to avoid

Posting more often helps, but **going overboard backfires**.

**1. Don't copy-paste the same text three times**
Each post should say something different. Repeating identical wording feels spammy. Change the content as you move from tease to reminder to launch.

**2. Posting hours early rarely works**
A morning post saying "stream at 20:00 tonight" will be forgotten by evening. Start your announcement sequence about 30 minutes before go-time. If you do post early, follow up with a reminder closer to the start.

**3. Don't let your feed become nothing but announcements**
An account that only posts stream links looks like a promo bot. Mix in casual thoughts, reactions to other streamers, and replies to listeners. A balanced feed keeps followers engaged between streams.

---

## Wrapping up

- Split announcements into **three posts: 30 min before, 5 min before, and at go-time**
- Use the templates so you're **not writing from scratch each time**
- Match your start time to **when your followers are online**

Before your next stream, jot the three templates into a note app. Copy, tweak a few words, and you're set — your announcement game just leveled up.
