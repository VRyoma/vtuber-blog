Mid-stream, a viewer asks something unexpected and your character reacts in a way that doesn't quite fit — and you know it. Vague character settings make it hard to stay consistent, especially under pressure.

A character bible fixes this. Writing one from scratch is daunting, but running through it as an AI dialogue is different: you just answer questions. Four prompts and you have a working document.

**What you will learn**

- Why a character bible keeps your streams consistent
- The four-step AI prompt flow
- How to use the finished document for scripts, replies, and updates

---

## Why a character bible pays off

### Fewer in-stream inconsistencies

When you know how your character would react in a given situation, you don't have to improvise from scratch. Collabs, unexpected questions, live events — a reference document makes fast, consistent decisions easier.

### Shared context with your audience

When your character's background shows up in your profile and stream introductions, viewers build a clearer mental model. That shared understanding also reduces wild variation in fan art and fan-made content.

### Reusable for all your AI prompts

Any time you use AI to draft stream scripts, comment replies, or social posts, paste the key fields from your character bible at the top. The tone and vocabulary stay consistent across everything.

---

## The four-step AI prompt flow

Run these in order. After each AI response, you can steer with "make it more [X]" or "less [Y]."

### Step 1 — Seed the session with your basics

```
I want to build a character document for my VTuber persona.
Based on the info below, ask me 10 questions that help develop the character further.

Name: [character name]
Appearance notes: [hair color, outfit, etc. — brief]
Content type: [gaming / karaoke / variety, etc.]
Vibe: [energetic / calm / tsundere, etc.]
```

### Step 2 — Answer the questions

AI will ask things like "What is this character best at?" and "What do they secretly hate?" Answer quickly and honestly — AI will expand and organize your answers.

### Step 3 — Get the formatted document

```
Based on our conversation, create a character document covering:

- Name, age, and origin (or world setting)
- Core personality traits (3–5 points)
- Catchphrases and recurring expressions
- Words or topics the character would never say (off-limits list)
- Likes and dislikes
- Backstory (1–2 paragraphs)
- How this character relates to their audience
```

### Step 4 — Stress-test it with roleplay

```
Stay in character as the persona we just built.
Respond to this situation: "A viewer asks your age mid-stream, out of nowhere."
```

Reading the AI's response tells you immediately where the character feels off. Every "that doesn't sound right" moment is a note to refine.

---

## Using the finished document

### Paste it into every AI draft prompt

When you generate a stream script or comment template with AI, put the personality section (traits, catchphrases, off-limits list) at the top of your prompt. The output will stay in character without extra effort.

### Use it as the source for your profile page

The "likes, backstory, and relationship to viewers" section can feed your channel's About page or the script for your debut / self-intro stream. Most of the writing is already done.

### Review it every three months

After a few months of streaming, some settings will feel unused or off. A quarterly review keeps the document honest — update it to match how the character has actually evolved on stream.

## Summary

- **A character bible reduces inconsistency and works as a reusable AI prompt layer.** Building it through dialogue is faster than writing from scratch — you just answer questions
- **Four steps:** seed with basics → answer AI's questions → export formatted document → stress-test with roleplay
- **Roleplay reveals the gaps.** Use realistic stream scenarios to spot what needs adjusting before it comes up live
