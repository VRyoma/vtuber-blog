You know who you want to collab with. You just don't know how to bring it up. "Want to collab sometime?" rarely gets a yes because the other person has no idea what you're picturing.

A one-page proposal fixes that. It lowers the barrier for both sides: you stop procrastinating, and they can actually evaluate the idea. AI drafts the proposal for you in 10 minutes.

**What you will learn**

- Why a written proposal improves your collab success rate
- A two-step prompt flow: proposal body + DM pitch message
- How to send it without coming on too strong

---

## Why a written proposal works better than a DM alone

### They can actually say yes or no

"Want to collab?" puts all the work on the other person. A proposal that says "1-hour stream, 3 rounds of [game], simulcast on both channels" gives them something concrete to respond to.

### Your intent comes through clearly

A short DM leaves too much ambiguous. When you write out the purpose — "I want our audiences to discover each other," or "I want to close out this streaming season with something special" — the other VTuber understands what they're walking into.

### Rejections become useful data

When someone says no, a proposal makes it easier to understand why. "The timing doesn't work, but the concept sounds fun" is actionable feedback. You can refine and re-pitch later.

## The two-step AI prompt flow

### Step 1 — Build the proposal

Fill in the brackets and run this prompt.

```
I want to create a collab proposal to send to another VTuber.
Using the info below, write a structured one-page proposal.

[My channel]
Channel name: [your name]
Content type: [gaming / karaoke / variety, etc.]
Approx. subscriber count: [1,000 / 5,000, etc.]
Audience profile: [horror game fans / late-night viewers, etc.]

[Their channel]
Channel name: [their name]
Their content type: [gaming / karaoke, etc.]
Why I want to collab with them: [similar audience / same game, etc.]

[Collab idea]
What we would do: [game match / song session / variety chat, etc.]
Estimated stream length: [1 hour / 2 hours, etc.]
Where it streams: [simulcast on both channels / one channel, etc.]

Include these sections:
- Collab title
- Goal (1–2 sentences)
- Format and rough rundown (bullet points)
- Mutual benefit
- Suggested dates (placeholder)
- Notes
```

### Step 2 — Generate the outreach message

```
Based on that proposal, write a short DM I can send to them.
- Around 80–100 words
- Friendly but not pushy — leave room for them to decline easily
- Briefly describe the concept and tell them the full details are in the attached proposal
```

## What good AI output looks like

### Title examples

The kind of collab titles AI tends to suggest:

```
"First-Time Horror Showdown: Who Screams First?"
"Song Relay — We Trade Favorites for 30 Minutes"
"Viewer Crossover: We Introduce Each Other's Audiences"
```

Don't love the angle? Just say "make it feel more [X]" and iterate.

### The mutual benefit section

This is the part that often makes or breaks a pitch. Write it honestly:

```
Example:
- Your audience gets introduced to their channel (and vice versa)
- The archive stays searchable long after the stream ends
- Collabs tend to attract new viewers who follow neither of you yet
```

## How to send it

### Share via Google Doc, not a wall of DM text

Paste the AI output into a Google Doc and share the link. A single-link DM is much easier to read than a 500-word message. The proposal feels more professional, too.

### DM first, proposal second

Send a short message first: "Would you be open to hearing about a collab idea I have?" Wait for a reply before sharing the doc. Leading with the link before they've agreed to look at it can feel like a lot of pressure.

### When they say no

"Doesn't work right now" is a perfectly normal answer. Reply with "No worries — hopefully another time" and move on. Don't push for reasons. The proposal will still be there when you reach back out next season.

## Summary

- **A written proposal gives the other person something concrete to evaluate.** It turns "want to collab?" into a real conversation
- **Two-step flow:** generate the proposal body, then generate a short outreach message
- **DM first, doc second.** Let them opt in before sending the full proposal — it feels less like homework
