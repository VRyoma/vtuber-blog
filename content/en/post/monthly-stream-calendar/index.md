Ten minutes before going live, you're still asking yourself: "What should I stream today?"

When you decide on the spot every time, your content gets lopsided and your schedule turns unpredictable.

This guide walks you through planning a full month of streams with Google Calendar and Sheets. Spend 30 minutes at the end of each month, and next month's streams fall into place.

Here's what you'll learn:

- Three concrete benefits of a monthly stream calendar
- A starter template for streaming three times a week
- Step-by-step setup with Google Calendar + Sheets
- What to do when your plan falls apart

## Three benefits of planning ahead

**Benefit 1: No more "what do I stream today?" panic**

Planning your content in advance means you can focus on the stream itself, not on scrambling for a topic. Assign "Tuesday = zatsudan (free-talk streams)" and "Thursday = gaming," and you'll shave 10 to 15 minutes off your prep time each session.

**Benefit 2: Viewers can find you more easily**

A regular time slot makes it easy for viewers to add you to their weekly routine. Many streamers report that first-time viewers stick around more often when there's a predictable schedule rather than random start times.

**Benefit 3: Your stream count stays steady**

You know the pattern: streaming every day in week one, then vanishing in week three. A calendar makes that imbalance visible so you can fix it. Target three streams a week, and you'll consistently hit around 12 streams per month.

| Benefit | What changes |
|---|---|
| Less day-of indecision | Title and prep time drop by 10–15 minutes |
| Easier for viewers to find you | A fixed slot tells them exactly when to show up |
| Stable frequency | You can maintain roughly 12 streams per month |

## A three-times-a-week starter template

First, pick your three days. Many growing streamers in the 1,000 to 50,000 subscriber range stream three to five times a week.

Two popular three-day patterns:

- **Mon / Wed / Fri** — even spacing, easy rhythm
- **Tue / Thu / Sat** — good if you want one weekend slot

Assign a genre to each day and prep gets even easier. Here's an example using Mon / Wed / Fri:

| Day | Genre | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Zatsudan (free talk) | 21:00–22:30 (90 min) |
| Wednesday | Gaming | 20:00–22:00 (120 min) |
| Friday | Utawaku (karaoke stream) | 21:00–22:00 (60 min) |

The key: **don't pack every single day**. Leave one or two days empty as a buffer. If you get sick or lose motivation, you can shift a stream to a buffer day without blowing up the whole plan.

## Setting up Google Calendar + Sheets

All you need is a free Google account.

### Step 1: Create a stream-only calendar

In Google Calendar, go to "Other calendars" and pick "Create new calendar." Name it something clear like "Stream Schedule." Keeping it separate from your personal calendar lets you view only your streaming plans at a glance.

### Step 2: Draft the month in a spreadsheet

Make a new Google Sheet with these columns:

| Column | What to put | Example |
|---|---|---|
| A: Date | Stream date | 2026-05-04 |
| B: Day | Day of week | Mon |
| C: Genre | Content type | Zatsudan |
| D: Title | Working title | Let's chat about May goals |
| E: Start time | Go-live time | 21:00 |
| F: Notes | Prep reminders, collab partners | Guest TBD |

Sit down for about 30 minutes at the end of the month and fill in the next month. Titles can be rough drafts — you'll tweak them on the day anyway.

### Step 3: Add events to Google Calendar

Work through your spreadsheet and create calendar events. For slots that repeat at the same time each week, use the "repeat" feature. A recurring "every Monday at 21:00" event covers you for up to a year in one entry.

### Step 4: Share the schedule with viewers

Once your calendar is ready, post the first week (or the whole month) on your profile or pinned post. Even sharing one week ahead helps viewers plan around you.

## When the plan breaks — and it will

Plans fall apart sometimes. That's normal. A few adjustment tricks keep things manageable.

1. **Keep one or two buffer days each week.** They're your safety net for rescheduling. If a buffer day stays empty, no guilt — it worked as insurance.

2. **Swapping genres within the same week is fine.** If Wednesday's gaming stream turns into an utawaku, that's okay as long as you still hit your three slots.

3. **Tell viewers early when you need a break.** Canceling the day of hurts trust more than posting a heads-up the day before.

4. **Review at the end of the month.** Check your spreadsheet: how many streams did you actually do? Which genre showed up most? Use those answers to shape next month's plan.

| What goes wrong | What to do |
|---|---|
| You're sick | Move to a buffer day, or drop to two streams that week |
| Can't pick a game | Swap the genre to zatsudan |
| A collab invite comes in last minute | Add it to a buffer day, or replace an existing slot |
| Motivation dips mid-month | Scale back to twice a week — don't push through burnout |

## Quick recap

- A monthly calendar eliminates day-of indecision, smooths out your frequency, and helps viewers find you.
- Build a three-day weekly template, assign genres, and leave buffer days open.
- Use Google Calendar and Sheets — 30 minutes at month's end is all it takes.

**Try this today:** Open Google Calendar and add just three stream slots for next week. Titles can be placeholders. Three entries are enough to make next week feel more under control.
