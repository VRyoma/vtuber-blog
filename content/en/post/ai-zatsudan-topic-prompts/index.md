Opening a search tab ten minutes before stream, wondering what to talk about? You worry about repeating old topics and end up with the same safe subjects every time.

The fix: outsource your brainstorming to AI. With the right prompt, you get a different set of topics for every broadcast.

**What you will learn**

- The three-part prompt structure that pulls high-quality topics from AI
- Three ready-to-use prompts with sample output
- How to adapt AI output so it sounds like you on stream

## Why AI brainstorming beats Googling for zatsudan (free-talk) topics

Finding topics for zatsudan streams eats more time than you think. Even with a running note, your ideas tend to cluster around the same themes.

ChatGPT and similar tools can help, but typing "give me chat topics" produces generic results. To get great output, give your prompt **three elements**:

1. **Role** -- who the AI should answer as (e.g., "variety show writer")
2. **Constraints** -- audience, time slot, tone (e.g., "solo VTuber, 5 minutes per topic")
3. **Output format** -- list, table, script (e.g., "numbered list of 10")

Combine these three and the quality jumps. Below are three prompts built on this structure.

## Prompt 1: Situation-Based Topic Generator

The most versatile prompt. Adjust the time slot and audience to get a fresh batch every stream.

```text
You are a variety show writer.
Generate 5 topics for a solo VTuber's zatsudan stream under these conditions:

[Conditions]
- Time slot: evening (9 PM or later)
- Audience: people in their 20s-30s, office workers and students
- Each topic should fill 5-10 minutes of talk
- Friendly to newcomers and regulars alike
- No politics, religion, or adult content
- Mix evenly across "daily life," "hypothetical scenarios," and "what-if" categories

[Format]
Numbered list. Add one line of notes after each topic explaining why it works.
```

**Sample output**

1. **"Something you bought recently that was totally worth it"** -- Works for online or convenience-store finds. Ask viewers to share their own recent purchases.
2. **"One app you would take to a desert island"** -- The reasoning reveals personality. Great for follow-up questions.
3. **"You have 500 yen at a convenience store right now. What do you buy?"** -- Budget constraints spark creative answers and lively chat.
4. **"If you could be another VTuber for one day, who?"** -- Can spin into a full impression segment.
5. **"I like something -- guess what it is in three hints"** -- Turns chat into a guessing game.

Swap the categories ("food," "gaming," " nostalgia") and you get different results every time. Adjust conditions for morning streams ("today's breakfast") or weekend streams ("how do you spend your day off?").

## Prompt 2: Five Icebreakers That Welcome New Viewers

Use this when new faces show up and you want a comment-friendly vibe. The prompt prioritizes questions anyone can answer in a word or two.

```text
You manage a streaming community.
Create 5 icebreaker questions that make first-time viewers want to comment immediately.

[Conditions]
- Every question can be answered in one word or a short phrase (e.g., "ramen," "dog person")
- Nothing that could lead to personal or identifying information
- Include 2 questions that work with a simple yes or no
- Warm, welcoming tone
- After each question, add a sample line the streamer can say to invite comments

[Format]
Numbered list 1-5. Each item has the question followed by one example line the streamer can read out.
```

**Sample output**

1. **"Are you a morning person or a night owl?"** -- "Drop your answer in chat! I'm definitely a night owl."
2. **"Describe your mood right now with one emoji"** -- "No need to type words. Just one emoji is fine."
3. **"What are you drinking right now?"** -- "I've got coffee here. How about you?"
4. **"What was your first impression of me?"** -- "Tell me what you thought when you clicked in -- I want to know!"
5. **"Favorite season -- in one word"** -- "Just one word is okay. Mine is summer."

Pick the one that matches your mood and run with it. New viewers often freeze because they do not know what to say. A low-barrier question like "morning or night?" makes it easy to type a quick reply.

## Prompt 3: Simple Questions That Spark Deep Conversations

For when your regulars keep coming back and you feel like you are recycling topics. This prompt generates questions that start simple but open up into surprisingly deep discussions.

```text
You are a radio show director.
Create 5 topics for a VTuber's zatsudan stream that look ordinary at first glance but lead to deep conversations once you start talking.

[Conditions]
- Base topics on everyday observations or small choices
- No single right answer -- topics should split opinions
- Easy for the streamer to mix in personal stories
- Should feel natural to open with "I was thinking about this lately..."
- Add two "expansion hints" per topic

[Format]
Numbered list. Each item has the topic followed by two hint lines.
```

**Sample output**

1. **"Do you have any habits you do without realizing?"**
   - Hint: Share one of your own habits first. Chat will jump in with "I do that too!"
   - Hint: Give concrete examples ("cracking knuckles," "touching your nose") to get laughs.

2. **"The thing you always end up buying at a convenience store"**
   - Hint: Reveal your go-to purchase ("I always grab two rice balls") to build familiarity.
   - Hint: Ask chat "what would you buy right now?" to keep comments flowing.

3. **"A misunderstanding from childhood you believed for way too long"**
   - Hint: Slightly embarrassing stories work great. Example: "I thought milk was cheap everywhere because Kyushu has lots of cows."
   - Hint: Ask chat to share theirs -- the thread can go on for a while.

4. **"Desert island: smartphone or a book?"**
   - Hint: A forced binary splits the room and heats up chat.
   - Hint: Follow up with "tell me why" and you learn what your viewers value.

5. **"Something you recently thought was surprisingly useful"**
   - Hint: Turns chat into a tip-exchange session.
   - Hint: Share your own discovery first so viewers feel safe contributing.

This prompt suits streamers with a loyal audience who feel their topic well running dry. Try "habits you do without realizing" -- within two minutes your chat will be buzzing.

## Three Steps to Make AI Output Sound Like You

Reading AI topics word-for-word on stream feels stiff. Three quick adjustments make the material yours.

**1. Jot down your own answer before going live**

When you get the topics, write one line of your own response for each. If the topic is "desert island app," write "YouTube -- I want to watch videos to kill time." Having your answer ready means you talk naturally without reading the screen.

**2. Attach a verbal habit**

Pick a phrase you already use and stick it on the front or back. "I'm curious about something" or "what do you guys think?" turns AI copy into your voice with zero effort.

**3. Plan your follow-up for when chat goes quiet**

Decide in advance what to do with short replies and silence. Short answers like "dogs" get a follow-up: "Oh, a dog person! What breed?" When nothing comes in, say "while everyone's thinking, I'll share my answer." Having a silence plan makes dead air far less stressful.

- Adapt AI output with your own experience, a verbal habit, and a follow-up plan
