Do you keep forgetting something different before every stream? The BGM volume one day, the OBS scene switch the next?

When prep feels chaotic, you rush to go live. Then you spend the whole stream worrying about what you might miss next. The fix is simple: **keep one checklist that matches your setup**.

This post shows how to feed your streaming setup into an AI and **generate a personalized checklist**. Copy the prompt, fill in your details, and you are ready to go.

**What you will get from this post:**
- Three common patterns behind stream-prep slip-ups
- A ready-to-use prompt template for generating checklists
- Example checklists for chat, gaming, and singing streams

---

## Three patterns behind most stream-prep mistakes

Prep errors tend to fall into a few categories. Knowing which one is yours makes the fix obvious.

### 1. You know what to do, but the order changes every time

Launch OBS, pick BGM, upload the thumbnail, review your opening talking points. You know all the steps, but do you do them in the same order every time?

Without a fixed order you keep doubling back: "Wait, I haven't done that yet." A five-minute prep routine can stretch to fifteen just from context-switching.

### 2. Different stream types need different steps

A chat stream, a gaming stream, and a karaoke stream (known as *utawaku* in the VTuber community) each have their own requirements. Gaming means checking game capture and framerate. Singing means ordering your tracks and balancing audio levels.

If you use the same generic checklist for everything, **critical items get buried inside steps that don't matter** for that format.

### 3. New plans make you forget the basics

Collabs and special events steal your attention. That's when the simple stuff slips through: unmuting your mic, double-checking the stream key, turning on notifications.

When you focus on something new, your brain skips the routine. That is exactly why a checklist that includes the basics matters.

## The prompt template for generating your checklist

If you just tell an AI "make a stream-prep checklist," you get a decent generic list. But to get something that fits **your actual setup**, share your tools and formats. Something as small as mentioning "OBS Studio on YouTube" instead of just "streaming" sharpens the output a lot.

Copy the prompt below and replace the bracketed details with your own.

```
You are a VTuber's streaming assistant.
Create a pre-stream preparation checklist based on these conditions.

[Streaming Setup]
- Streaming software: OBS Studio
- Platform: YouTube
- Audio: VTube Studio + VoiceMeeter
- Other tools: Streamlabs (alerts and notifications)

[Frequent Stream Types]
- Chat stream (about 3 times a week)
- Gaming stream (about once a week)
- Singing stream (about once a week)

[Output Format]
- Group items by stream type
- Add a checkbox ([ ]) to each item
- List items in the order they should be done
- Group by timing: "30 minutes before," "5 minutes before," etc.
```

### How to customize the prompt for yourself

1. **Streaming setup** — List every tool you actually use. Specify the exact version if it matters ("Streamlabs OBS, not regular OBS").
2. **Stream types** — Only include formats you run regularly. Add a collab or event row if you do those.
3. **Output format** — Asking for checkboxes means you can print the list or paste it straight into a notes app.

Treat the AI output as a **first draft**. After a few streams, add items you missed and cut the ones that were unnecessary. The list grows into something genuinely yours.

## Three example checklists by stream type

Below are sample outputs you might get. Adapt them to your own setup.

### Chat stream

| Timing | Check item |
|---|---|
| 30 min before | Schedule stream in YouTube Studio (title, description, thumbnail) |
| 30 min before | Open OBS, confirm scene is set to "Standby" |
| 30 min before | Play BGM and check volume (around -25 dB is a good target) |
| 15 min before | Unmute mic, record a short test clip and check audio |
| 15 min before | Place today's topic notes on your desktop |
| 5 min before | Post "starting soon" on social media |
| 5 min before | Confirm Streamlabs alerts are turned on |
| Go live | Start stream, wait 10 seconds, switch from "Standby" to "Chat" |

### Gaming stream

| Timing | Check item |
|---|---|
| 45 min before | Launch the game and check for updates |
| 30 min before | Confirm OBS game capture is picking up the game window |
| 30 min before | Record a test clip to check game audio vs. mic balance |
| 15 min before | Check framerate stability (OBS stats panel) |
| 15 min before | Hide any UI elements you don't want on screen |
| 5 min before | Post "starting soon" on social media |
| Go live | Start stream, ask chat if the game feed looks correct |

### Singing stream (*utawaku*)

| Timing | Check item |
|---|---|
| 45 min before | Finalize your set list and song order |
| 30 min before | Confirm every track file plays without errors |
| 30 min before | Adjust desktop audio-to-mic ratio in the OBS audio mixer |
| 15 min before | Play the first track and record a test clip to check balance |
| 15 min before | Verify lyrics overlay or lyrics tool is working |
| 5 min before | Warm up your voice (light vocal exercises, stretching) |
| Go live | Start stream, switch to your greeting scene before the first song |

## How to refine your checklist over time

The AI gives you a starting point. The real value comes from **updating it after every few streams**.

### 1. Note what you forgot right after stream

While the stream is still fresh in your mind, jot down anything you missed. Even one line is enough. Add those items before your next session. After three or four rounds you will have covered nearly all your personal blind spots.

### 2. Keep it to 10-15 items

Once a checklist passes twenty items, reviewing it becomes a chore. Cut yours down to **10-15 essentials**. A good filter: "Would the stream fail without this?" Move nice-to-have items to a separate list so your pre-stream routine stays tight.

### 3. Ask AI for a review every month or so

After about a month of using your list, feed it back to the AI along with your current stream formats. Ask whether anything is missing. You will get fresh suggestions you might not have thought of.

Any time your setup changes (new mic, different platform, updated software), update the prompt and regenerate.

- Most prep slip-ups fall into three buckets: inconsistent order, format mismatch, and basic-step amnesia under new conditions.
- A short prompt describing your setup and stream types gives you a **custom checklist in about five minutes**.
- Treat the list as a living document. Add post-stream notes, trim to **10-15 items**, and revisit with AI every month.
