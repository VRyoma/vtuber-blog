"Tonight's stream felt kind of off." You know the feeling — but without turning it into something specific, nothing changes by next week. The same patterns repeat because vague dissatisfaction doesn't translate into different behavior.

Hand AI three answers about your stream and it returns a specific action to try next time. The whole process takes under 5 minutes.

**What you'll get from this:**
- Why "felt off" never becomes improvement without language
- A three-question prompt you can copy and use tonight
- How to turn five reviews into a personal pattern file

---

## Why "Felt Off" Never Becomes Better

### The memory fades fast

A "something was wrong" feeling after a stream fades within 24 hours. By the time your next stream rolls around, you've lost the specifics. You repeat the same patterns because you can't recall what to change.

### Vague fixes don't change behavior

"I should be more energetic next time" or "I should prepare better" aren't actionable. Contrast: "Write one page of notes on the opening 5 minutes before the stream starts." That second version can actually be done. The gap between them is specificity — and AI is very good at generating specific language from vague feelings.

---

## The Three-Question Prompt

Copy this after your next stream and fill in the brackets.

```
You're a VTuber stream coach.
Help me review today's stream.

Stream type: [variety / game commentary / karaoke / other]
What felt like it worked: [e.g., the game intro section went smoothly]
What felt like it didn't work: [e.g., ran out of things to say in the last 30 min]

Please give me:
1. Why the thing that worked probably worked (1–2 sentences)
2. A hypothesis for why the thing that didn't work went wrong (1–2 sentences)
3. One specific action to try in the next stream
```

Input takes 1–2 minutes. The response comes back in under a minute. Total time: 3–5 minutes.

---

## Reading the Review

The response comes in three parts.

### Part 1 — Why it worked

AI names the likely mechanism behind what went well. "The game intro felt smooth because you gave viewers enough context to understand the situation" is more useful than just knowing it went well, because now you can repeat the specific behavior intentionally.

### Part 2 — Why it didn't work (hypothesis)

This is the highest-value part. "Ran out of things to say" might come back as "possibly because there was no prepared fallback material for the second half." You don't need the hypothesis to be exactly right — you just need it to be plausible enough to act on. If it resonates, use it. If it doesn't, discard it.

### Part 3 — The one action

This is the only thing that actually needs to change before next stream. Copy it directly into your prep notes.

```
[Next stream action]
"Write 3 lines of closing material to use at the 45-minute mark"
```

One line at the top of your prep notes changes what you walk into the stream with.

## Building a Weekly Routine

Running a review after every single stream isn't sustainable when you're tired after streaming. Start with once a week.

### Weekly routine structure

| When | Task | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Immediately after stream | Jot one "worked" and one "didn't work" | 2 min |
| Next day | Paste into the prompt and send it | 3 min |
| Before the next stream | Re-read last week's one action | 1 min |

The "immediately after stream" note is the critical one. The specifics fade fast — writing one line while you're still in the session is much easier than reconstructing it the next morning.

---

## After Five Reviews: Build Your Own Template

Five reviews will surface patterns. You'll start to see which situations you consistently handle well and which ones keep breaking down.

### What five reviews reveal

- What always works (your strengths to lean into)
- What keeps going wrong (the recurring obstacle)
- What conditions require specific preparation

Once you see the pattern, add it to the prompt:

```
[My pattern notes — from 5 reviews]
- Strength: game intro sections always get positive comments → keep doing them
- Recurring issue: last 30 minutes, topics run dry → stock 3 topics for the second half
- Trigger: streams over 1 hour need a closing routine prepared in advance
```

With this context added to your prompt, the AI review accounts for your specific history instead of giving generic advice.
