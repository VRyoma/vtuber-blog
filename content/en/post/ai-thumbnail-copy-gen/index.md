You finish the thumbnail design, then you stare at the text layer for ten minutes. The words never come as easily as the visuals.

AI can fix that. Give it your video details and it outputs 10 tagline options in one shot. Your job shrinks to "pick the best one and tweak it."

**What you will learn**

- Why thumbnail text drives click-through more than the image does
- A prompt that outputs 10 options in the right format
- How to choose and refine the winner

---

## Why thumbnail text is the real click-trigger

### Viewers read text before they process the image

YouTube thumbnails are small. Fine details get lost, but large text pops even on a phone screen. If that text makes someone think "I need to see what happens," they click. If it doesn't, they scroll past.

### Keep it under 10 characters (Japanese) — or around 4–6 words in English

Short taglines read at a glance. "BOSS DOWN," "NO WAY," "Almost Quit" — one phrase that captures the moment. Anything longer competes with the visual for attention.

---

## The prompt for 10 tagline options

Fill in the brackets and paste this straight into your AI chat.

```
Generate 10 thumbnail taglines for a YouTube video.

Video content: [one or two sentences describing the highlight]
Genre: [gaming / karaoke / variety, etc.]
Target viewer: [people who play this game / VTuber fans, etc.]
The main moment: [clear / fail / emotional scene, etc.]

Rules:
- Each tagline is 6 words or fewer
- Every option should trigger "curiosity," "surprise," or "emotion"
- Mix sentence fragments, exclamations, and questions
- Symbols (! ? …) are fine
```

---

## Reading the output and picking a winner

### Sample output (last-boss-clear video)

What AI typically suggests:

```
① No way that worked
② I actually beat it
③ Refused to give up
④ The impossible clear
⑤ I almost quit here
⑥ One hit left…
⑦ Tears incoming
⑧ That ending though
⑨ It's finally over
⑩ Never stop trying
```

### Three criteria for your pick

**1. Does it match the thumbnail image?**
A crying face next to "IMPOSSIBLE CLEAR" clashes. "Tears incoming" fits. Match the emotion in the image and the text — they should say the same thing.

**2. Does it sound like you?**
Read it out loud. If "The impossible clear" feels stiff, try "I almost quit here" instead. Your tagline should fit your personality.

**3. Can you slot a searchable keyword in?**
If you need a keyword like "first clear" or "final boss," put it in smaller supporting text on the thumbnail. Keep the main tagline emotion-first.

---

## Refining the winner

### Push it from 85 to 100

If the best option is close but not quite right, tell AI what to tweak:

```
Take "Refused to give up" and make it feel more emotional.
Give me 5 variations.
```

### Use font size to give it punch

Once the words are locked, split the phrase across two sizes. "Refused" small, "to give up" big — the reader's eye moves, and the impact sticks.

---

## Summary

- **Thumbnail text often decides the click more than the image does.** Short, punchy phrases outperform detailed visuals at small sizes
- **One prompt gets you 10 options.** Pass in your video content, genre, and key moment — then pick, don't create from scratch
- **Match image, voice, and optional keyword.** Once you have a winner, one more AI pass can sharpen it further
