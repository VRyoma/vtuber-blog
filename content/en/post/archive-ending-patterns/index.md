You wrap up the game, say "okay, I think that's it for today," and close the stream.

Repeat that enough times and you'll notice something: viewers enjoy the stream in the moment, but they don't have a strong reason to come back next time. That "see you next week" feeling has to be built — it won't happen on its own.

**What you'll take away from this post:**
- The most common ending patterns that hurt repeat viewership
- Three stream endings you can try tonight
- A quick guide for matching the ending to your stream style

---

## Why "Just Ending" Costs You Repeat Viewers

### What viewers are thinking as your stream ends

The final minutes of a stream are when viewers decide, without thinking too hard about it, whether they'll show up next time. Three questions run in the background:

- "Was today worth it?"
- "Do I know when the next stream is?"
- "Is there a reason to come back?"

Answer any one of those clearly and you've given them a reason to return. Leave all three unanswered and you're relying on habit alone — which is fragile, especially for newer viewers.

### Three endings that work against you

| Pattern | Typical words | Why it hurts |
|---|---|---|
| Exhaustion ending | "Okay I'm wiped, let's call it" | No energy, no forward momentum |
| Fade-out ending | Stream just winds down mid-conversation | Leaves viewers feeling cut off |
| Sudden stop | "Out of time, bye!" | Jarring — viewers feel abandoned |

The exhaustion ending is the most common. It's honest, but it leaves nothing behind. Viewers say "good work!" in chat, and then there's nothing pulling them back next week.

## Three Stream Endings Worth Using

All three take under two minutes. Pick one and try it tonight.

### Ending 1: Next-Stream Preview

Before you close, spend thirty seconds telling viewers what's coming next — the date, the time, and one specific thing they can look forward to.

> "Next stream is Saturday at 9pm. Planning to finish this playthrough and then try a no-damage run. Hope to see you there."

The specific detail matters. "See you next time" is forgettable. "No-damage run on Saturday" gives viewers something to look forward to. Even a rough timeframe — "sometime next week" — is better than nothing.

### Ending 2: Today's Highlight Recap

Before signing off, pick one or two moments from the stream and call them out.

> "That took two and a half hours but the boss fight at the start — chat went wild. And the route ○○ suggested actually worked, which I did not expect."

This does two things. First, it gives the stream a satisfying close, like the last beat of a good story. Second, viewers who missed the stream will want to check the archive to see what you're talking about.

Keep the recap to one or two minutes. Any longer and it starts to feel like the stream can't end.

### Ending 3: A Question for Chat

End by asking viewers one question — something they can answer in the comments or on social media, and something you'll actually respond to next stream.

> "Still deciding what to play next. Drop your vote in the comments — option A or option B. I'll go with whatever gets more votes."

When viewers leave a comment, they're invested in the outcome. That investment brings them back to see what you chose.

The key is that the question needs a payoff. "What do you think?" doesn't create a follow-up. "Vote for next game and I'll announce it at the start of next stream" does.

---

## Matching the Ending to Your Stream Style

| Stream style | Best ending | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Game streams | Ending 1 or Ending 3 | Viewers can follow the story arc or vote on direction |
| Chat / variety streams | Ending 2 or Ending 3 | Recapping the conversation extends the warm feeling |
| Karaoke streams | Ending 1 or Ending 2 | "That last song" moments drive archive replay |

Ending 3 works in almost every situation. If you're not sure where to start, that's the one to try first.

---

## Building It Into a Habit: A One-Week Routine

Knowing the patterns isn't enough — you need to make them automatic. The way to do that is to use the same ending every stream for seven days straight.

### A 3-minute note after each stream

Right after you close the stream, spend three minutes on three quick notes:

- **Best moment today** (for the highlight recap)
- **What's happening next stream** (for the preview)
- **One question worth asking viewers** (for the chat question)

With those notes ready, you won't be scrambling for an ending while you're still on screen. By day four or five, the pattern starts coming naturally.

After the week, look at which ending got the most response from your audience. Double down on that one, and adjust from there.

---

## Takeaways

- Ending a stream without intention leaves viewers with no reason to return
- Pick one of the three endings and use it tonight
- After seven streams with the same pattern, it becomes yours

If you want the simplest possible starting point: use Ending 3. Ask chat "option A or B for next game?" before you close. That's a two-sentence change with real impact.
