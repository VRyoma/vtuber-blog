You found a great indie game on sale, but your stream title is just "[Game Name] Playthrough" again. A title like that tells viewers nothing about why they should click.

This article shows you how to **use AI to summarize a game's appeal in three short lines**, then turn those lines into stream titles and descriptions. You'll get a ready-to-paste prompt and three worked examples.

**What you'll learn:**

- Three reasons indie game stream titles end up bland

## Why Indie Game Stream Titles Fall Flat

Indie game names rarely hint at what you actually do. "Hollow Knight" and "Celeste" are well-known, but the titles alone tell you nothing about the genre. AAA games benefit from genre shorthand like "FPS" or "RPG." Indies don't have that advantage.

Three patterns cause bland titles:

1. **The name alone says nothing about the experience** -- "Playing Iron Lung" doesn't tell you it's a horror game about piloting a submarine through an ocean of blood.
2. **Genre tags get skipped** -- writing "indie puzzle" means little when viewers aren't familiar with the scene.
3. **The "what happens" part is missing** -- most titles list the game name and stop, instead of hinting at a moment or feeling.

### Bland vs. Compelling: Side by Side

| Genre | Bland | Compelling |
|---|---|---|
| Horror | [Night Signal] Playthrough | [Night Signal] The radio picked up... a dead person's voice? Horror indie first play |
| Puzzle | Playing an indie puzzle game | Flip the cover and the world turns inside out -- puzzle indie 1-hour clear challenge |
| Adventure | [A Short Hike] Mountain climbing game | All you do is reach the summit, and somehow it makes you cry -- hand-drawn adventure one-shot |

### Three Before/After Pairs

**Pair 1: Creepy adventure**
- Before: "Playing Iron Lung"
- After: "Piloting a sub through an ocean of blood -- short horror Iron Lung"

**Pair 2: Platformer**
- Before: "Celeste playthrough"
- After: "Controls are jump and dash. That's it. And it's still brutally hard."

**Pair 3: Story-driven puzzle**
- Before: "Bought an indie game, gonna play it"
- After: "Change the past and the future vanishes -- time-leap puzzle Unpacking"

Adding one sentence about what happens in the game makes your title stand out in search results. The "Before" examples above are just game names. The "After" examples describe an experience. Let's look at how to get AI to write that sentence for you.

## How to Get AI to Write a 3-Line Game Hook

The idea is simple: **give the AI the game's name and a short description, and ask it to squeeze the appeal into three lines**. You want punchy phrases that can double as stream titles, not long paragraphs.

### Step 1: Copy-Paste Prompt

Paste the following prompt into any AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) and fill in the brackets.

```
You are a copywriter who specializes in indie game appeal.
Write a "3-line hook" for the game below, suitable for stream titles.

Game: [game name]
Genre: [puzzle / horror / adventure / action / etc.]
Playtime: [1 hour / 3 hours / 5+ hours / etc.]
One-line note: [paste the store page description, or jot down 1-2 things that caught your eye]

Rules:
- Each line: 20 words or fewer
- Say what happens and why it's fun -- be specific
- Don't end a line on a vague word like "amazing" or "fun" alone
- Each line should highlight a different appeal
```

One advantage of this prompt: **the "one-line note" field accepts a raw store page description**. You don't have to summarize the game yourself. One paste and you're done.

### Step 2: Sample Output (Outer Wilds)

**Input:**

```
Game: Outer Wilds
Genre: Exploration adventure
Playtime: 15-20 hours
One-line note: A space exploration game where you investigate a solar system trapped in a 22-minute time loop
```

**AI output:**

1. Every 22 minutes the universe resets -- where do you go next?
2. Every clue is already there from the start -- solve the mystery in any order
3. Only knowledge carries over -- progress comes from insight, not skill

Each line contains a phrase you can drop straight into a stream title. "Every 22 minutes the universe resets" and "only knowledge carries over" are both title-ready.

If the output doesn't click, try these follow-up prompts:

1. "Shorter -- 15 words max per line" -- tighter phrasing that fits a title
2. "Make it punchier" -- pushes the AI toward stronger emotional words
3. "Add searchable genre keywords like horror or puzzle" -- helps with discoverability

You only need one usable line from the three. You don't have to use all of them.

## Turning the 3 Lines into Titles and Descriptions

Once you have the three lines, pick the catchiest one for your title and put the other two at the top of your video description.

### Three Title Patterns

Build your title using one of these templates.

**Pattern 1: Catchphrase + Game Name**
- "The universe ends every 22 minutes -- Outer Wilds first playthrough"
- The most versatile pattern. Viewers searching the game name will still find you.

**Pattern 2: Question + Genre**
- "What's a time loop where only knowledge carries over? -- Exploration adventure"
- Titles that end with a question mark tend to get more clicks.

**Pattern 3: One-Sentence Experience**
- "Everything vanishes every 22 minutes -- solving a time-loop mystery"
- Drops the game name and leads with the experience itself.

### From 3 Lines to Titles: Another Example

**AI output (Pentiment):**

1. A murder hidden in a medieval manuscript -- chase the truth across 25 years
2. Every choice reshapes the townspeople's fate -- who do you save, who do you abandon?
3. A world that looks like a hand-painted art-history textbook -- the text alone pulls you in

**Title ideas:**

1. "Chasing a murder across 25 years -- Pentiment first playthrough" (Pattern 1: Catchphrase + Game Name)
2. "Every choice changes someone's fate -- medieval mystery Pentiment" (Pattern 1, variant)
3. "A hand-painted mystery that makes you cry over text -- Pentiment one-shot" (Pattern 3: Experience)

The line you lead with changes the tone. "Murder" hooks curiosity. "Fate" tugs at emotion. Pick the phrase that feels most natural to say on stream.

### Using the Remaining Lines in Your Description

The two lines you didn't use in the title work well at the top of your video description. YouTube shows the first two to three lines in search results, so placing the game's appeal there gives it more visibility.

**Example description opening:**

```
A world that looks like a hand-painted art-history textbook.
The text alone pulls you into a medieval mystery.

Game: Pentiment
Genre: Adventure / Mystery
Stream length: ~2 hours
```

Pasting the AI output as-is is already better than leaving the description blank.

## Wrapping Up

- Give an AI the game name and a store description, and it will summarize the appeal in three usable lines.
- Pick the catchiest line, drop it into a "catchphrase + game name" template, and you have a stream title.
- Paste the remaining two lines into your video description -- done.

2. Paste it into the prompt above and let the AI generate a 3-line hook.
3. Use the best line as your title, paste the rest into your description.
