## 1. Three Time Traps in Karaoke Stream Setlist Planning

These are the most common ways song selection eats your evening.

### Trap 1: Spending 15 minutes just picking a theme

You start wondering, "What vibe should today's stream have?" Before long you are scrolling through old streams and playlists. The root cause is usually too many open-ended choices.

**Fix:** Write down five or six theme ideas ahead of time. On stream day, pick one and go. Good examples: "heartbreak songs," "high-energy anime ops," "showa-era covers," "holiday set." Keep them broad enough to match any mood or season.

### Trap 2: Rearranging song order over and over

You picked the songs, but now you are stuck. "Is a ballad right after this one too heavy?" "Are three uptempo tracks in a row exhausting?" Five or six reorder passes later, you are still not done.

**Fix:** Split the setlist into four blocks: opener, development, climax, closer. Assign two or three songs to each block. That gives you a natural arc without the guesswork.

### Trap 3: Dropping songs because you are not sure you can sing them

"This one has a high chorus..." You second-guess yourself, cut the song, and go hunting for a replacement. That loop burns another ten minutes.

**Fix:** Note your comfortable singing range (the keys where you sound best) ahead of time. Feed that into the AI prompt and it will suggest songs that fit your voice from the start.

## 2. The AI Setlist Prompt You Can Copy and Paste

Paste this prompt into ChatGPT, Claude, or any AI assistant. Replace the `[ ]` brackets with your own details and you are ready to go.

```
You are a karaoke stream setlist assistant.
Suggest a setlist based on these conditions:

[Theme]: [e.g., heartbreak songs, summer anime ops, relaxing covers]
[Stream length]: [e.g., 60 minutes]
[Song count]: [e.g., 10-12 songs]
[Comfortable key]: [e.g., female songs +2 to +3, male songs at original key]
[Avoid]: [e.g., songs I have sung 3+ times, tracks with very high notes]
[Must include]: [e.g., YOASOBI "Idol" / YOASOBI "アイドル", Aimyon "Marigold" / あいみょん "マリーゴールド"]

Output format:
1. Song order (opener -> development -> climax -> closer)
2. Artist name for each song
3. Recommended key changes (e.g., +2)
4. Best spots for an MC break
5. Total estimated runtime including MC breaks
```

### Sample output (theme: heartbreak songs, 60 minutes)

The AI might return a setlist like this:

1. **Opener** — "Leo / Kenshi Yonezu / 米津玄師" (original key)
2. **Opener** — "Shape of Love / SEKAI NO OWARI" (+2)
3. **Development** — "Marigold / Aimyon / あいみょん「マリーゴールド」" (+1)
4. **Development** — "Sayonara no Kyou ni / Aimyon / あいみょん「さよならの今日に」" (original key) -- MC here (1 min)
5. **Development** — "Zankyou Sanka / Aimer / 残響散歌" (-1)
6. **Climax** — "Yoru ni Kakeru / YOASOBI / 夜に駆ける" (+2)
7. **Climax** — "Shinunoga E Wa / Fujii Kaze / 死ぬのがいいわ" (original key)
8. **Climax** — "Idol / YOASOBI / アイドル" (+3) -- MC here (1 min)
9. **Closer** — "Usseewa / Ado / うっせぇわ" (+2)
10. **Closer** — "Kakurenbo / Yuuri / 優里「かくれんぼ」" (original key)

Estimated runtime: about 55 minutes (including two 1-minute MC breaks)

Notice how the AI maps songs to a four-block structure. That saves you from starting from scratch every time.

## 4. How to Tweak AI Suggestions for Your Voice

AI output is a starting point, not the final answer. Adjust these three things to make the setlist work for you.

### Tip 1: Give specific details about your comfortable range

Instead of just "female songs +2," tell the AI something like "my chest voice tops out at C5, falsetto reaches E5." That helps it pick safer keys. Measure your range once with a piano app and keep the note handy. It only takes about five minutes.

### Tip 2: Tell the AI to avoid back-to-back high songs

Three high-energy tracks in a row will strain your voice. Add one line to the prompt and the AI will space them out with lower-key songs or ballads.

```
[Extra condition]: No more than two high-note songs in a row (chorus peak at C5 or above).
```

### Tip 3: Give the AI feedback after you try the setlist

If a song felt too high or the energy dipped in the middle, tell the AI and ask for a revised list. Small requests like "lower song 2 by one semitone" or "swap songs 5 and 6" work fine. Two or three rounds of back-and-forth usually produce a setlist you can actually use.

## Wrap-up

Three takeaways for using AI as your setlist assistant:

- **Cut time waste** by keeping a short list of theme ideas and your vocal range notes ready before you start.
- **Paste your details into the prompt** and get an opener-to-closer structure in seconds.
- **Sing through the output and tweak it.** Two or three rounds of feedback give you a practical, voice-friendly setlist.

If you can shrink a 30-minute song-picking session into three to five minutes, you gain that time back for practice or social media promotion. Try the prompt at your next utawaku (karaoke stream) and see how it feels.
