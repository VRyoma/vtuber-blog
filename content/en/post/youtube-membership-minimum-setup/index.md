You want to start channel memberships, but you keep putting it off. "I do not have enough perks lined up." "My subscriber count feels too low." If any of that sounds familiar, you are in good company.

The truth is, three perks and one low-price tier are enough to open memberships today. This article covers the five-step setup using a single price tier, plus three things to check when members are not signing up.

## When Is the Right Time to Start Memberships?

Feeling like "it is too early" when you have fewer than 100 subscribers is normal. But once your channel meets YouTube's monetization requirements, you can enable memberships at any time.

What matters is not assembling perfect perks before you launch. It is **launching first and improving as you go.** Even one member paying a small monthly fee is meaningful, recurring support.

### A quick checklist before you start

If you can answer yes to these three questions, you are ready.

1. **You stream at least once a week** — you have a foundation for member-exclusive content.
2. **Some viewers already say they want to support you** — you see encouraging comments or Super Chats (paid highlighted messages that appear during streams).
3. **You can prepare something for members once a month** — the workload does not overwhelm you.

Subscriber count is just a rough indicator. If the three points above apply, you can launch memberships with five subscribers or fifty.

## Three Perks Are All You Need

Piling on perks increases your workload and makes consistency harder. Start with these three.

### 1. Create five to ten custom emojis

Custom emojis are the easiest and most effective membership perk. Chat-only emojis give members a shared identity and make the chat feel like a private space.

- Set five to ten emojis on your lowest price tier.
- Good themes include common phrases from your streams, your character's expressions, and inside jokes your community already uses.
- Free assets and image generators can produce them in one to two hours.

### 2. Publish one members-only archive per week

Take part of your stream archive and make it available only to members. You do not need to record anything new. Past streams, unbroadcast segments, and outtake compilations all work.

- Set aside the last 30 minutes of a chatting stream as a members-only archive.
- Collect funny fails or bloopers from a gaming stream into a single video.
- Upload one short behind-the-scenes update each month.

### 3. Run one 30-minute members-only stream per month

Host a stream that only members can join. Keeping it to once a month limits the preparation burden.

- A 30-minute to one-hour casual chat is plenty.
- Good topics include Q&A, personal updates, and upcoming stream plans.
- Pick a fixed day and time so members can plan around it.

### Balancing effort and appeal

| Perk | Setup effort | Ongoing effort | Appeal to members |
|---|---|---|---|
| Custom emojis | Low (one-time) | Almost none | High |
| Members-only archives | Low | Low | Medium-High |
| Monthly members-only stream | Medium | Medium | High |

Emojis offer the best return because you create them once and they keep working. Members-only streams take more ongoing effort, but they deepen the bond with your audience.

## Five Steps to Set Up Memberships

Follow these steps inside YouTube Studio.

### Step 1: Enable monetization

Open YouTube Studio and click **Monetization** in the left sidebar. If your channel qualifies, you will see a **Membership** tab. If monetization is not enabled yet, apply for it first.

### Step 2: Turn on memberships

Click **Get Started** in the Membership tab. Review the terms and policies, then accept them.

### Step 3: Set your price tier

Start with the **lowest available price** — on most markets that is around the equivalent of a snack or a coffee. A low tier lowers the barrier for first-time supporters.

- One low-price tier is enough at launch.
- Add higher tiers later if demand appears.
- Splitting perks across tiers adds management complexity, so keep everything on one tier at first.

### Step 4: Register your perks

In the **Levels** screen, add the three perks described above.

1. **Emojis** — Upload PNG files with transparent backgrounds. YouTube recommends 48 x 48 pixels.
2. **Members-only community posts** — When writing a Community post, set the visibility to "Members only."
3. **Members-only streams** — When scheduling a stream, toggle the "Members only" setting on.

### Step 5: Announce it on stream

Once everything is set, mention memberships on your next stream. Keep these three points in mind.

- **Explain what members get in one sentence** — "For the price of a coffee each month, you get custom emojis and a members-only stream once a month."
- **Show viewers where the join button is** — Point to the "Join" button below the video player on screen.
- **Do not push too hard** — Saying "only if you can afford it" tends to build more goodwill than a hard sell.

Thirty seconds at the end of a stream is enough. Repeat the announcement on every stream and awareness will grow over time.

## Three Things to Check When Sign-Ups Stall

It is common for memberships to start slow. If members are not joining, review these three areas.

### 1. How often and when you announce

Are you mentioning memberships during streams? Viewers miss announcements that only happen once.

- Include a 30-second plug in every stream, at least once a week.
- Mention memberships near both the opening and closing of a stream.
- Post a Community update about memberships once a month.

### 2. How you describe your perks

Saying "I do members-only streams" states a fact but does not give viewers a reason to join. Add specifics.

- Tease the topic of a recent members-only stream: "Last month we talked about X behind the scenes."
- Use thumbnails and titles for members-only archives that spark curiosity.
- With permission, read a member's comment on a public stream to show the community vibe.

### 3. How easy it is to join

Small frictions — a hard-to-find join button, an unclear process — can stop interested viewers from signing up.

- Add a "About memberships" link in your stream description.
- Write a short membership pitch in your channel's About section.
- Thank new members by name on stream to make the experience feel personal.

## Summary

- **Three perks — custom emojis, members-only archives, and one monthly members-only stream — are enough to start.** You do not need a long perks list.
- **One low-price tier and five setup steps** get your memberships live today.
- If sign-ups stall, review your **announcement frequency, how you describe your perks, and how easy the join process is.**

**Try this today:** Open YouTube Studio, go to the Monetization tab, and check whether memberships are available. If they are, create your lowest-price tier and upload one custom emoji. That is your starting point.
