You stream consistently, but something isn't clicking. Views are flat, watch time is low, and you can't tell why. You open YouTube Analytics, see a wall of numbers, and close it again.

AI can parse that data for you. Paste in your weekly stats and ask for three specific things to test next week. The whole process takes 15 minutes and builds into a useful habit fast.

**What you will learn**

- Which numbers to collect and where to find them
- The weekly review prompt template
- How to turn the output into real, testable changes

---

## What data to collect

### From YouTube Studio

Pull these once a week. Go to **YouTube Studio → Analytics → Content** to see per-stream numbers.

**Per stream:**
- View count (not impressions — different number)
- Average watch duration (or average view percentage)
- Like count
- Comment count
- Live chat messages

**Channel-level (that week):**
- Total watch hours
- Subscriber change (net gain / loss)
- Impressions click-through rate (CTR)

### Your own notes

Raw numbers miss context. Track these too:

- Game or topic for each stream
- Start time and stream length
- Moments that felt lively (memorable viewer reactions)
- Any technical issues

## The weekly AI review prompt

Fill in your data and paste it in.

```
I'm a VTuber trying to improve my streams week over week.
Analyze this week's data and tell me what to improve.

[This week's streams]
Stream 1: [game/topic] / Views: [N] / Avg watch time: [Nmin] / Comments: [N] / Start: [time] / Length: [Nhrs]
Stream 2: [game/topic] / Views: [N] / Avg watch time: [Nmin] / Comments: [N] / Start: [time] / Length: [Nhrs]
(add more rows as needed)

[Channel totals this week]
Total watch hours: [N]
Subscriber change: [+N / -N]
Impression CTR: [N%]

[My notes]
Best-feeling stream this week: [name]
Stream that felt flat: [name]
Technical issues: [yes/no — describe if yes]

Analyze with these questions in mind:
1. What's different between the strong streams and the weak ones?
2. What likely caused the low average watch times?
3. Give me 3 specific things to test next week.
```

---

## How to use the output

### Pick 1–2 actions, not all 3

AI might suggest things like "open with a clear statement of what the stream is about within the first 30 seconds" or "add your character name to the thumbnail text." These are concrete and testable.

Don't try all three at once. When you change too many things, you can't tell what actually moved the needle. Test one change, check the numbers the following week, then add the next.

### Average watch time is your best single metric

View count can spike from a good title even if the stream itself wasn't engaging. Average watch time reflects whether people stayed. If it's low (say, under 3 minutes for a stream), viewers are clicking in and bouncing quickly.

When you find a stream with high watch time, ask AI why:

```
My stream [title] had an average watch time of [N min], the highest this week.
Based on everything you know about that stream, what 3 things might have driven that?
```

### Stack four weeks for a monthly picture

After four weeks of data, the monthly trend becomes visible. Run this prompt:

```
Here are four weeks of data. Identify:
- What has clearly improved
- What hasn't changed
- What has gotten worse
One or two items per category is enough.
```

## Making it a habit

### Lock in a day and time

"Every Monday before I prep for the week's streams" works well. Tying the review to prep keeps them connected — you review last week while planning this week.

### Keep a running spreadsheet

A Google Sheet with one row per week takes two minutes to update and gives you a visual trendline you can scroll through. Paste those rows into your AI prompt when you're ready.

### Analysis should serve the stream, not replace it

If the numbers become the point, streaming stops being fun. Think of the weekly review as a support tool — a way to get your content to more people who'd enjoy it. When numbers dip, the right response is usually a small tweak, not a full reset.

---

## Summary

- **Paste your YouTube stats into AI and get three actionable improvements in 15 minutes.** View count, average watch time, comments, and a few personal notes are all you need
- **Test one or two changes per week, not all three.** Isolating variables gives you real data, not noise
- **Average watch time is your most honest metric.** It shows whether people stayed, not just whether they clicked
