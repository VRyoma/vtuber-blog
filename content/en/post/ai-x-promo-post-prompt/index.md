Thirty minutes before stream. You are staring at the compose box, wondering what to write. That ritual adds up faster than you think.

When the post you finally wrote gets barely any engagement, the next one feels even harder. That is the cycle this prompt breaks.

Here is how to **feed your own best-performing posts to AI and get promo drafts that sound like you**.

**What you will learn:**
- How to write a prompt that captures your voice
- Three promo templates for before, during, and after stream
- A three-point checklist so you never post raw AI output

---

## Writing promo posts from scratch every time is exhausting

These three pain points come up a lot:

1. **You keep reusing the same phrasing.** Variety runs dry.
2. **Emoji placement and line breaks take forever.** Layout eats time.
3. **You cannot tell why one post works and another flops.** No review loop.

Writing from scratch takes about five to ten minutes each time. Stream four times a week and that is 80 to 160 minutes a month. You could spend that time on prep or just resting.

The fix: use your own high-performing posts as source material. Give those to AI and it can generate variations that keep your tone.

---

## A prompt that teaches AI your tone

If you just say "write a promo post," AI gives you something stiff and generic. The trick is to **show it how you write before asking it to write**.

### Prompt structure

Bundle these four elements and send them together.

```
# Role
You are a promo-post assistant for a VTuber on X (formerly Twitter).

# Tone Reference
Below are promo posts that performed well in the past.
Study the phrasing, emoji use, and line-break rhythm.

[Post 1]
Live at 9 PM tonight -- casual chat stream!
Gonna show off some merch I've been obsessed with.
Fellow oshi-activity folks, come hang out!
#VTuber #casualstream

[Post 2]
Stream is ON!
Trying a horror game... haven't cleared a single stage yet.
Help me out T_T
[stream link]

[Post 3]
Thanks for coming, everyone!
Next stream: Friday 9 PM, karaoke!
Taking requests in the comments!

# Rules
- Casual, spoken style -- no stiff politeness
- Two or three emojis max per post
- One or two hashtags
- Use line breaks to create a natural rhythm
- One exclamation mark per line at most

# Task
Using the stream info below, write three variants of a
{pre-stream / mid-stream / post-stream} promo post.

Stream info:
- Date & time: {e.g. April 22, 9 PM JST}
- Content: {e.g. indie game first playthrough}
- Highlight: {e.g. first time playing this one}
```

### How to use it

1. **Gather three to five strong past posts.** Pick ones with high likes or reposts.
2. **Paste them into the Tone Reference section.** Replace the placeholder posts.
3. **Update only the Task section each stream.** Everything else stays the same.

Save the whole block in a notes app. Swap the stream info and you are ready to go.

## Three promo templates for every stream phase

Your promo should shift depending on where you are in the stream. Ask AI to generate for each of these timings.

| Timing | Goal | What to include | Length guide |
|---|---|---|---|
| 30 min before | Build awareness | Start time, content teaser, call to join | 80--120 chars |
| Right after start | Drive clicks | "We're live," one-line hook, link | 60--100 chars |
| After stream | Bridge to next | Thanks, highlight, next-stream teaser | 80--120 chars |

### Generated examples

**30 minutes before:**
> Casual chat at 9 PM!
> Today's theme: "Things I'm obsessed with lately."
> Drop your own current obsessions in chat too.
> Starts at 9 -- be there!

**Right after start:**
> We're LIVE!
> First-time indie game playthrough.
> Not sure I can clear this... come watch me struggle.
> [stream link]

**After stream:**
> Thanks for hanging out today!
> Next up: Friday 9 PM karaoke.
> Taking song requests now!

In the Task section, switch `{pre-stream / mid-stream / post-stream}` to generate all three from the same stream info in one shot.

## Three things to check before you post AI output

Treat every AI draft as a rough sketch. Before you hit post, run through this list.

**1. Words you would never say**

AI likes to sneak in formal phrasing or vocabulary you never use. Watch for things like "please be sure to join" or "we cordially invite you." If it feels off, rewrite it in your own voice.

**2. Wrong facts**

Start times, titles, links -- AI can mix these up. Double-check dates and times against your actual schedule. "Tonight at 9" turning into "Tomorrow at 9" is more common than you'd think.

**3. Energy level mismatch**

AI copies tone but struggles with intensity. A huge announcement might come out flat. A casual chat post might sound over-the-top. Read it once out loud and adjust the energy to match the content.

This three-point check takes one to two minutes. Compared to writing from scratch for five to ten minutes, the time savings add up fast.
